Homework Tips

  • We've heard comments that the homework problems are not reflective of the quizzes. When writing the quizzes, we match every single quiz question to a homework question. The homeworks are similar in difficulty to the quizzes, but they may feel easier because you can talk to your friends, ask a TA for help, etc. This is why we encourage you to work on homeworks by yourself, and to discuss with friends and attend office hours only after having struggled with the problems on your own.
  • If the 6 hour time limit is a problem for you, you may want to reassess your perspective towards homework. Homework has little weight in the final grade. It exists to prepare you for the quizzes and final. Therefore, don't stress about getting 100% on the homework. If you make two submissions and earn 80%, then you will be fine as long as you learned the material. The additional submissions are just a bonus to help you verify your understanding.
  • Conversely, just because you earned 100% on a homework does not necessarily mean you understand the material well enough, especially if you required substantial help.

Quiz Tips

  • You don't need to solve every problem to earn an A. However, the quizzes are designed so that you should have no problem doing at least 70% of the quiz if you understood the homework. (Yes, even Quiz 3.)
  • When you start the quiz, spend two minutes reading the problems. Choose 4 or 5 problems to focus on, and ignore the rest. Come back to the remaining problems at the end if you have time.
  • As you may have noticed, we only award credit for progress towards the final answer. You are unlikely to earn any credit by attempting a problem that you don't know how to do. Focus your energy on problems you know how to do, and do them well.
